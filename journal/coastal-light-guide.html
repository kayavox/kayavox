Coastal Light: A Collector's Guide

Collecting art is not about filling walls. It is about curating feelings — choosing pieces that transform the way a space breathes.

1. Choosing with Intention

The best collectors don't buy impulsively. They live with a question: what feeling do I want this room to hold?

Tip: Before purchasing, imagine the piece at 7 AM with morning light, and at 9 PM with a lamp. If it resonates in both, it belongs.

2. Placement

Eye level is the golden rule. Center the artwork at 57-60 inches from the floor.

Give it breathing room. Leave 6-8 inches between the frame and furniture. Silence amplifies signal.

Consider the light path. Position impasto art where light can interact with its texture.

Tip: For rooms with limited natural light, use a picture light angled at 30 degrees.

3. Framing

Matte black or walnut for museum-quality. Natural wood for organic coastal. Float frames for contemporary.

"The frame is the border between the art and the world."

4. Paper Quality

We recommend 210g/m² matte fine art paper. The matte surface absorbs light, enhancing the illusion of texture. Avoid glossy paper.

5. Building Over Time

Start with one piece that resonates. Live with it. Then add a companion — not matching, but responding. A dawn on one wall, a dusk on another.
